10 Critical Issues

These sections create genuine legal risk for Guldmann and must be resolved before this handbook is distributed to UK employees.

  • 1
    Salary deduction for driving fines ERA 1996 s.13

    The handbook states Guldmann "may deduct from your salary" for speeding fines. A handbook statement alone is insufficient - the Employment Rights Act 1996 s.13 requires a specific contractual provision or the employee's prior written consent. Current wording creates an unlawful deduction risk.

  • 2
    Holiday entitlement references EU / Danish law WTR 1998

    The handbook cites the EU Holiday Act and the Danish vacation year (1 September - 31 August). The EU Holiday Act no longer applies in the UK post-Brexit. UK employees are entitled to 28 days under the Working Time Regulations 1998 - not 20 as implied.

  • 3
    5-day qualifying period for sickness during holiday WTR 1998

    The 5-day qualifying period before reclaiming annual leave for sickness is Danish law and does not exist in the UK. UK employees can reclaim from day 1 of sickness under the Working Time Regulations 1998 (Stringer v HMRC [2009]). This clause may unlawfully deny statutory rights.

  • 4
    Maternity and parental leave - entirely absent for UK MPLR 1999

    The maternity / parental leave section contains only Danish-language leave tables in Danish. There is no UK content at all. UK entitlements (52 weeks maternity leave, SMP rates, paternity leave, Shared Parental Leave) are completely missing.

  • 5
    Statutory Sick Pay not mentioned SSP / ERA 2025

    The Danish municipal sickness benefit system is described as if it applies to UK employees. It does not. UK employees are entitled to Statutory Sick Pay (GBP 116.75/week). From April 2026, SSP is payable from day 1 under the Employment Rights Act 2025.

  • 6
    Parental Bereavement Leave entirely absent PBLA 2018

    The Parental Bereavement (Leave and Pay) Act 2018 is not referenced anywhere. On the loss of a child under 18, employees have a statutory right to 2 weeks leave from day 1 of employment plus SPBP at GBP 187.18/week. One Guldmann Day Off does not satisfy this Act.

  • 7
    Flexible working statutory right missing ERFW Act 2023

    Since 6 April 2024, all employees have a day 1 statutory right to request flexible working under the Employment Relations (Flexible Working) Act 2023. The handbook presents flexible working as entirely at managerial discretion with no mention of this right.

  • 8
    Pension auto-enrolment not described Pensions Act 2008

    The entire pension section is placeholder text ("pension scheme xx... insert link"). The Pensions Act 2008 requires auto-enrolment of eligible employees (aged 22-66, earning over GBP 10,000/year) with a minimum 3% employer contribution. None of this is described.

  • 9
    Dependant care leave is a Danish placeholder ERA 1996 s.57A

    The child/dependant care leave section lists Danish entitlements (2 care days per year for children under 14) and is marked "[insert rules for UK]". The Employment Rights Act 1996 s.57A right to time off for dependants is completely absent.

  • 10
    Untranslated Danish language text throughout

    Multiple sections contain untranslated Danish: sick leave ("Alt sygefravaer registreres"), company car section ("De samme regler gaelder i udlandet"), and the entire parental leave table (Mor, Graviditetsorlov, Barselsorlov, Faedreorlov, uger). These sections are legally meaningless to UK employees.
